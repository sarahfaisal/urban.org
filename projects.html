Project: Gender Sensitive Messaging

Aimed at Building Respect for Empowered Gender Roles in Children


Introduction

Pakistan ranked 153rd out of 156 countries in the Global Gender Gap Report 2021, ranking seventh out of eight South Asian countries, with Afghanistan being the lowest. In the 2019 Women, Peace and Security Index, Pakistan ranked 164th out of 167 countries. What is even more concerning is that gender disempowerment is happening despite that fact that legislations and related legal instruments do exist in Pakistan that cater to the protection of women.

This publication aims to contribute to creating a discourse centered around ‘Gender Sensitive Messaging’ that targets young minds—school children. It is felt that working with this age group and coming up with communication tools and models that can have a widespread impact can influence long-lasting change in building a new generation founded on respect for women and comfort in their empowered role. Such an intervention can also help in preventing our youth from going down other destructive paths, leading to violence, hatred, and intolerance.


This publication is a pilot medium to communicate gender sensitive messaging for children, contextualized within a pedagogical experience, with the aim of inculcating respect and acceptance for empowered gendered roles. This illustrative output is a result of extensive secondary research that looked into developing an understanding of ‘gender empowerment’.

Secondary research was followed by structured primary research, where the project team engaged with faculty of selected schools, child psychologists, and social scientists in Karachi to learn about different models of learning experiences. Subject research led to content development and design where concept and preliminary drafts were shared for feedback with collaborating schools. Draft contents were also tested under supervision of faculty to get target audience response. This feedback was incorporated into the final design product.

Deliverables

The publication works with a variety of engaging content that includes stories, interactive exercises, and information. It is structured in a way that can facilitate interactive engagement with students in creative learning formats.

The long-term objective is to have this publication act as a ‘pilot’ that can then lead to Gender Sensitive Messaging becoming a part of the learning experience, finding a space alongside the formal teaching curriculum.

The publication will be distributed in local city schools, selected civil society organizations, and shared with elected representatives, policymakers, and relevant government departments. It will also be disseminated through digital media to a wider audience.

Project Sponsorship

This project was sponsored by the Friedrich Naumann Foundation and has been enriched by collaboration with Habib University's Office of Research and Graduate Curation School Program.

Project: BATH ISLAND RESTORATION

GULSHAN-E-FAISAL PARK, STREET 17


Project Background

ST 17 plot in Bath Island was envisioned as a ‘public park’ in the Bath Island Scheme developed in the early 70’s. The park is nestled between the formal Bath Island settlement and the informal settlement of Hijrat Colony (originally an unauthorized settlement that was later regularized).

The space has been upgraded at various stages since its inception, with the last upgrade being done about ten (10) years back but renovation works have never been successfully sustained and as of now the park is in a state of disrepair and not equipped to attract any regular visitation by residents.

Presently, it only serves as a space at times frequented by youth of Hijrat Colony for playing cricket, or as a short cut route for pedestrians and motorcyclists moving in and out of Hijrat Colony. There is now a community led effort to restore that park where the Bath Island community has engaged with the Karachi Metropolitan Corporation (KMC) Parks & Horticulture Department and wishes to have the park restored in a participatory manner.

The team of Urban Collaborative has engaged with critical stakeholders – KMC and communities – both Bath Island and Hijrat Colony residents in order to ensure that the design is human centered and demand driven with critical stakes built and ownership created.

The research and design team of Urban Collaborative in collaboration with local community and KMC conducted a comprehensive secondary/primary research work that includes:

  • Site Analysis - Physical survey of the site/mapping
  • Qualitative and quantitative research – Engaging with communities (Bath Island/Hijrat Colony) by conducting In-Depth-Interviews, Focus Group Discussions, and Surveys to document user experience, perceptions and aspirations for improvement
  • Precedent Analysis


Findings of the Public Engagement Process have fed into the Design Development Project.


Project Goals and Objectives

  • Through innovative and thoughtful design options cater to communities belonging to both the different socio-economic profiles existing in the Bath Island and Hijrat Colony localities
  • Design should accommodate multiple uses and activities for different age groups, comfort of space and access and mobility ease that includes incorporation of Universal Access Design requirements
  • Design should build in considerations of cost effectiveness through accommodation for sustainable materials use and importance of durability (compatible to climatic condition of the project area – example reduced use or avoidance of metal and cement/concrete)


Target Audience

A participatory and collaborative engagement between key stakeholders is driving the whole process where the users of the space include residents of Bath Island and Hijrat Colony while the park space itself falls within the jurisdiction of KMC. The proposed design options would be presented to both the communities and KMC.


Analytical Framework

The research and design work in this project has placed its analysis and design within a strategic analytical framework that covers four important public space design thematic cores:

  • Uses & Activities
  • Comfort & Accessibility
  • Inclusivity
  • Ownership


Uses & Activities

A broad range of activities that cater to a diverse profile of visitors, across varying age groups and gender, is critical to defining a good public space. A space that offers uses and activities that draw visitors on a regular basis is important. Different activities can attract different crowds at different times of the day therefore the spatial set up and time slots for various activities and uses are important factors


Comfort & Accessibility

Public space designs that emphasize comfort are more likely to be perceived to be inviting. This means accounting for safety, cleanliness and seating arrangements aligned with appropriate natural or artificial shading. People prefer to have a choice in sitting and relaxing under a shade or being up and about; involved in various activities. A good public space design addresses also the basic concerns of a clean and litter-free area, a safe and secure atmosphere and the aesthetic appeal of the place. The space in terms of mobility should need to take special account of the mobility and accessibility needs of the elderly and people with special needs


Inclusivity

A sociable environment is a key attribute of any successfully designed public space. This can be measured by whether people feel comfortable and encouraged to meet their friends or interact with neighbors and even strangers within the space. Achieving this quality can be a difficult task but every successfully designed space will possess this attribute. A truly sociable space should depict diversity which is reflective of the population residing within its proximity


Ownership

The visitors should also have a sense of ownership towards the space such that they assume stewardship of it. It is therefore important to make the space design and implementation process participatory so that maximum stakes are built in it, so that once the space is activated all critical stakeho lders work together to sustain it.

Project: KARACHI, PUBLIC

A Collaboration of the People


Introduction

Karachi, Public (www.karachipublic.com) is an advocacy group that aims to center public interest with urban design perspectives supporting responsible urban, natural and  economic growth in Karachi. The “Envisioning Clifton Beach” pilot project aims to document the wealth of research and spatial imaginary of the public pertaining to the coast of Karachi – a rich ecological, social and financial resource of the city. This initiative is a response to the inaccessibility crisis at the public commons Clifton Beach, directed by Planner and Urban Designer, Rida Khan, and once developed will serve as a permanent public space for visioning, documenting, and consulting on the sustainability considerations of Karachi coast. Research and documentation support for this work in Karachi is being provided by the team of Urban Collaborative (www.urban.org.pk)

The first phase of the “Envisioning Clifton Beach” pilot is the research process where secondary and primary research data will be collected, analyzed and documented in the digital commons (karachipublic.com) on the Clifton Beach/Sea View coastal strip.

Project area is Clifton/Sea View Beach (see Diagram 1: Project area) and research work focus would have both secondary and primary research elements – gathering policy and legislative instruments, data and research work relevant to the project area and generating primary data – both qualitative and quantitative. Research work to include:

  • Creating graphics/illustrations with annotations in English/Urdu documenting existing
  • status across varying attribute sets and developing constraints and opportunities
  • scenarios
  • Producing video content
  • Communication on social media
  • GIS Mapping
  • Secondary and primary research (online and field based interviews/focus group
  • discussions and surveys)


Deliverables

Clifton Beach site surveying with pictorial documentation/mapping to form a catalog that would include:

  • Objects and activities that influence behavior, like lights, bins, benches, kiosks, barriers, art installations etc.)
  • Documenting mobility and circulation patterns including parking spaces (motorized and non-motorized) and space usage (mobile and stationary)
  • Surveying beach visitors – qualitative (interviews and focus group discussions) and quantitative (survey) for documenting views and perceptions on present visitation experience and future possibilities for Clifton Beach
  • Interviews with relevant experts and stakeholders
  • Analysis and design recommendations of survey feedback received up to date
  • Compile Clifton Beach + coast database on website library - will require searching and scanning for documents and maps across internet and physical libraries
