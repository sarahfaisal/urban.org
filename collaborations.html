Collaborations


A Green Agenda for Karachi City!

Photo courtesy: Crops in pots

Urban Collaborative is presently collaborating with Crops in Pots

(https://www.facebook.com/cropsinpot/) in rolling out an agenda for a green Karachi where multiple forms of urban forestation are explored and promoted for implementation in an inclusive, strategic and holistic manner.

Crops in Pots has initiated in the city the ‘Learning Garden’ concept and practice where organic gardening practices are introduced in local schools, while workshops are also conducted for general stakeholders.


Karachi Collaboration

At present Urban Collaborative is working with the Crops in pots team to develop a framework for action where a variety of urban forestation projects can be designed as tangible products where multiple stakeholders – government, civil society and private – can be engaged for sustainable implementation. Options under consideration include edible farming/rooftop and vertical, kitchen gardening with associated waste composting dividends, healing and therapeutically designed gardens etc. In addition, educational material (print and video) is also being looked into. There is also a focus on introducing heirloom seeds.

Placemaking – Inclusive Public Spaces for Inclusive Cities

Photo courtesy: Aesthetic Community Design

Urban Collaborative and Aesthetic Community Design (ACD) –(https://web.facebook.com/pg/betterbuiltcities/posts/) are engaging in promoting narratives, methodologies and practices of ‘Placemaking’ to explore how they can be adapted within the local context for innovations in public space design for making a more inclusive city.

Aesthetic Community Design (ACD) is a professional startup based in Karachi, Pakistan that aims to address Karachi’s qualitative ranking, moving from being the world’s least livable cities to that of the most livable. Achieving a healthy built environment involves a cultural shift on all levels of society from administrative and policymaking down to grass root community &amp; neighborhood levels. ACD plans to work on these aspects of sustainable social thinking for protection of neighborhood culture, through economic viability, environmental monitoring &amp; social aesthetics. ACD has developed a Design Intervention Kit for community mobilization on basis of spatial and sensorial order. In providing public with healthier alternatives, through sports, culture and creative activities in neighborhoods, ACD strongly believes public spaces such as streets, open spaces, parks, and common buildings in Karachi can act as a platform for developing grass root social infrastructure and that is where the importance of upgrading public spaces becomes imperative in overall physical development of a city like Karachi.


Karachi Collaboration

Urban Collaborative is engaging with Aesthetic Community Design in structuring strategies and urban design options for reviving and enhancing ‘neighborhoods’ – A focus on engaging with communities, elected representatives and service providers to rejuvenate the ‘neighborhood culture’ – making neighborhoods more walkable and cycling friendly, enhancing existing and innovating new vibrant gathering spaces to improve quality of living, strengthen community bonding and build social capital. A strong element of ‘volunteerism’ and ‘youth/student’ engagement will be embedded in neighborhoods selected for pilot interventions.
